Use alternative text for images in content

Content
Testing

People who can't see an image need some alternative to understand the content. You need to add "alt-text".

How to write alt-text for content

The content of the alt-text depends on the sort of image it is and its context. If the image is part of the main content of the page (as opposed to a functional image like an action link), the alt-text should describe the image in a way that makes sense in the context.

Imagine you were reading the page out to a friend. How would you describe the image?

You don't need to explain that it's an image as screenreaders usually announce that. Keep alt-text to a sentence or 2. Screenreaders may not read out alt-text longer than 250 characters.

This is an image from the NHS.UK bullous pemphigoid page. It comes under a heading "Check if you have bullous pemphigoid". The alt-text is: "Lots of sore red patches with small blisters spread across white skin on a woman's chest." It explains what users can see in the picture.

{{ image({ "src": "https://dummyimage.com/300x150/D8D8D8/D8D8D8", "alt": "Lots of sore red patches with small blisters spread across white skin on a woman's chest.", "caption": "It can affect large areas of the body or limbs." }) }}

The caption underneath the image assumes that the user can understand the image, either by seeing it or reading the alt-text. The caption shouldn't duplicate the alt-text. Instead, use it to explain why the image is there and what you want users to conclude from it - for example, how serious their symptom is or what stage their condition has reached.

Here are 2 more good examples. In the first one, the alt-text is "A bowl of fruit". It doesn't have a caption. Instead there's a link.

{{ image({ "src": "https://dummyimage.com/300x150/D8D8D8/D8D8D8", "alt": "A bowl of fruit", "caption": "" }) }}

In the second one, the alt-text is "Small red spots on white skin". The caption explains that that's how chickenpox starts.

{{ image({ "src": "https://dummyimage.com/300x150/D8D8D8/D8D8D8", "alt": "Small red spots on white skin", "caption": "1. Chickenpox starts with red spots. They can appear anywhere on the body." }) }}

If you have a complex image that you can't describe in short alt-text (such as an infographic), you need to include a longer description in some other way. You can use an expander, for example, underneath the infographic with a text explanation.

If your image has text which conveys its meaning, follow the guidance on functional images. An example would be a link to a health app which includes a brand image and the name of the app. The app name says the same as the brand image, so the image doesn't need explaining.
