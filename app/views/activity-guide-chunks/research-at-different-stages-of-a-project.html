User research

How people with different abilities can be involved at each stage varies, but as a general outline:

  • Interviews should include people with access needs.
  • Surveys should be conducted using accessible survey software. [We use SurveyGismo which is ok, as is SurveyMonkey. not sure if it's appropriate to recommend something here? typeform is awful, here are some anecdotal reviews.]
  • Testing paper or low-fidelity mock-ups can include people with hearing, communication, and dyslexia access needs.
  • Prototype code can also be tested with people with mobility, cognitive and vision (magnification) issues.
  • Production code should be testable with everyone, so adding people who use speech output, speech input, and colour-changing plugins.

James Buller from the Home Office did a talk on how they incorporated people at different stages of a project.
