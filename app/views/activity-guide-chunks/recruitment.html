Recruitment

User research

Many companies that recruit for UX research can include people with disabilities if asked, and GDS have published a few recruitment tips.

The exact mix of participants can vary depending on the type of research or interface being tested, but we generally try to include people with:

  • Learning and cognitive disabilities (e.g. dyslexia, dyscalculia, ADHD, or a learning or intellectual disability);
  • Limited mobility, someone who uses voice input or alternative method of input.
  • Low vision, someone who needs to adjust their computer such as using a screen magnifier or zoom controls;
  • No hearing, often it is useful to recruit a sign language interpreter at the same time, or even via the interpreter;
  • No vision, someone who needs to use a screen reader to use a computer or device;
  • It is not necessary to include all of these categories in one day, but reasonable coverage should be achieved through a project.
